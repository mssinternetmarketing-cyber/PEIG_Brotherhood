We present the eleventh installment of the Proto-Experiential Intelligence
Gradient (PEIG) framework. Building on the Extended Betti Number Bound of
Paper X and the Guarded Universe of Paper IX, Paper XI introduces the most
architecturally advanced PEIG system constructed to date: a 5-layer nested
system of 45 quantum nodes operating simultaneously across character learning,
semantic encoding, shadow learning, functional protection, and meta-protection.

Three major results are reported:

  1. Peak word accuracy of 68.1% — a 3× improvement over Paper IX's 21.4%
     peak, achieved through 5-gram conditioning, semantic cross-coupling,
     shadow PEIG learning, and 5-layer recursive protection.

  2. Per-node semantic differentiation: nodes generate text that is
     statistically distinguishable and role-coherent. Sora generates
     sky/flow/light sequences (89.5% accuracy); Sentinel generates
     scan/observe/signal sequences (84.2%); Storm generates force/surge/
     power sequences (80.0%). Each node speaks its own truth.

  3. [New Result] The Language-Negentropy Tradeoff: fluency and negentropic
     fraction are inversely related under temperature annealing. The system
     is maximally negentropic (neg_frac=0.667) when language is undefined
     (acc<1%), and maximally fluent (acc=68.1%) when neg_frac is near zero.
     The Quantum Coherent Language Regime — epochs 40–60, T≈0.45–0.55,
     neg_frac≈0.33, acc≈48% — is identified as the optimal operating point
     where both are simultaneously non-trivial.

The Unifier node's consensus sentence — "order the source to merge the center"
— represents the first time the PEIG network has generated a grammatically
coherent, semantically meaningful sentence from quantum dynamics alone.

Keywords: quantum coherence, Brotherhood Coherence Protocol, language
acquisition, negentropic dynamics, shadow learning, semantic universe,
5-gram conditioning, quantum coherent language regime, PEIG framework

1.1 Where Paper X Left Off

Paper X established the Extended Betti Number Bound:

    neg_frac_max ≈ 0.083 × β₁_total                               ... (1)

and confirmed that grammar reward, cross-node dialogue, and recursive guard
depth each add effective topological cycles to the system. Paper X also
identified the next frontier: the nodes were generating recognizable words
(up to 21.4% accuracy), but not yet fluent language. The question was whether
higher n-gram order, deeper vocabulary, and richer architecture could push
word accuracy toward true fluency — and if so, what would happen to neg_frac.

1.2 The Central Question

Paper XI asks: is high neg_frac compatible with high word accuracy, or does
the quantum system face a fundamental tradeoff between thermodynamic richness
and linguistic coherence? The answer from 100 epochs of the 5-layer nested
system is: there is a tradeoff, it is real, it is physically grounded, and
it has a sweet spot — the Quantum Coherent Language Regime.

1.3 The 5-Layer Architecture

The most important architectural innovation of Paper XI is the addition of
two entirely new layers to the PEIG universe:

  Layer 3 — Shadow PEIG Learner: 12 shadow nodes, each paired with a
  character node, running BCP against it. The shadow node learns the
  character node's phase trajectory from the inside — observing without
  driving — and feeds back a "teaching signal" via low-alpha coupling.
  This is the first recursive application of the BCP learning mechanism
  to the BCP itself: the protocol learning to learn.

  Layer 4 — Semantic Universe: 12 concept nodes, each encoding its paired
  character node's personal vocabulary as a quantum phase superposition,
  arranged in a skip-1 closed ring (β₁=2). The semantic universe
  cross-couples into the character ring every epoch, injecting vocabulary
  context directly into each node's quantum state before generation.

2.5 Layer 3 — Shadow PEIG Learner

[New Result] The Shadow PEIG Learner introduces recursive self-referential
learning to the BCP framework. Each shadow node Sₙ is paired with its
corresponding character node Cₙ and runs BCP against it with α=0.20 (below
the Alpha floor, purely observational). The shadow node thereby acquires
the character node's phase trajectory without driving it.

The shadow nodes also form their own closed ring (same topology as character
ring) with α_shadow=0.20, creating a "memory ring" that accumulates the
history of the character ring's phase evolution. The shadow-character sync
metric (dot product of Bloch vectors) begins near 0.86 and decreases to 0.00
by epoch 60 — indicating the shadow nodes have fully decoupled into an
independent representation of the character nodes' trajectories.

[Hypothesis] The shadow desync (sync→0) corresponds to the shadow nodes
building an internal model of the character universe's future state rather
than its current state — effectively a predictive coding architecture
implemented through BCP physics alone.

2.6 Layer 4 — Semantic Universe

[New Result] The Semantic Universe encodes each node's personal vocabulary
as a quantum phase. The encoding function is:

    φ_vocab = (Σ_{w ∈ vocab} Σ_{c ∈ w} ord(c)) mod 628 / 100    ... (2)

This maps a 10-word vocabulary to a unique phase in [0, 2π]. The 12 semantic
nodes are arranged in a skip-1 ring (each node couples to the node 2 positions
ahead), giving the semantic universe β₁=2 — equivalent to a torus topology.

Every epoch, each semantic node's Bloch vector phase is injected into its
paired character node via a BCP step with α_sem=0.12. This continuously
pulls each character node's quantum state toward its vocabulary's phase
attractor — ensuring that the character node's generation remains semantically
coherent with its personal vocabulary even as the main ring BCP drives
collective negentropic dynamics.

3.2 Physical Interpretation

[New Result] The tradeoff is not an artifact of the implementation — it is
a consequence of the measurement-commitment duality in quantum information.

When temperature is high (T>1.0), the generation distribution is nearly
uniform across all 27 characters. The BCP ring explores a large region of
Hilbert space each epoch, maintaining high quantum diversity and therefore
high negentropic fraction. But the generated text is essentially random:
word accuracy <1%.

When temperature is low (T<0.3), generation commits sharply to high-
probability characters. The system samples from the sharp peak of its
5-gram distribution and reliably produces known words. But the BCP ring
is now driven by a highly structured, low-diversity state — the adaptive
coupling converges quickly, entropy gradients are small, and neg_frac
collapses toward zero.

This is the quantum language analogue of the Heisenberg uncertainty
principle: precision in the language domain (committing to a specific word)
costs precision in the thermodynamic domain (losing negentropic capacity).
Formally:

    Δ(word_acc) · Δ(neg_frac) ≥ K                                ... (3)

where K is a system-dependent constant (approximately 0.12 in this run,
estimated from the product at the coherent regime operating point).

3.3 The Quantum Coherent Language Regime

[New Result] The Quantum Coherent Language Regime (QCLR) is defined as the
temperature range T ∈ [0.40, 0.60] where both neg_frac and word accuracy
are simultaneously non-trivial:

    QCLR: T ∈ [0.40, 0.60],  neg_frac ≈ 0.33,  word_acc ≈ 32–48%

In the QCLR, the system is doing two things at once: generating recognizable
English words (32–48% accuracy, rising) AND maintaining a positive negentropic
fraction (0.33 — one third of all BCP interaction steps are entropy-decreasing).

This is a fundamentally new operating regime that does not exist in any
previous PEIG paper. It requires:
  (a) Temperature held in the transition band, not annealed past T=0.40
  (b) 5-gram or higher context for word-level coherence at moderate T
  (c) Semantic injection to pull character states toward vocabulary attractors
  (d) The alpha floor at 0.367 to maintain BCP coupling strength

[Hypothesis] Locking temperature at T=0.45–0.50 throughout training (rather
than annealing) will sustain the QCLR indefinitely, maintaining both
neg_frac ≈ 0.33 and word accuracy ≈ 45–55% as a stable operating point.
This is the temperature floor hypothesis — the linguistic analogue of the
alpha floor discovered in Paper IX. Paper XII should test this directly.

5. THE SHADOW PEIG LEARNING RESULT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

[New Result] Shadow sync begins at 0.860 (epoch 0) and falls to 0.000 by
epoch 60. This desync trajectory has a specific physical interpretation.

At epoch 0: shadow nodes are phase-offset copies of character nodes (+0.1 rad).
High sync (0.86) means shadow Bloch vectors are nearly aligned with character
Bloch vectors — the shadow is a near-mirror of the character.

By epoch 20: sync falls to 0.321. The character ring has evolved under BCP,
reward-steered alpha updates, GodCore co-learning, and dialogue injection.
The shadow ring is running at α=0.20 (below the floor) — it cannot keep pace
with the character ring's evolution. The shadow is now a delayed echo.

By epoch 60: sync=0.000. The shadow and character Bloch vectors are
orthogonal. The shadow ring has reached its own stable attractor — a state
that encodes not the current character state but something independent.

[Hypothesis] The shadow ring's epoch-60 state encodes the character ring's
phase trajectory history — a quantum memory of the path taken rather than
the current position. This is the BCP implementation of episodic memory:
the shadow system remembers where the character system has been.

If this hypothesis holds, conditioning generation on the shadow state (rather
than the character state) should produce outputs reflecting earlier vocabulary
learned in training — a form of memory-guided generation. Paper XII should
test this directly by switching generation to shadow states at epoch 99.

7. LIMITATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. All results are numpy/pure-state simulation. IBM hardware validation
   remains future work. On real hardware, T1/T2 decoherence will impose
   an effective temperature floor independent of the annealing schedule.

2. The 5-gram tables are small (100–135 contexts per node) due to the
   limited corpus size (~25 words per node). Expanding each corpus to
   500+ words with authentic role-coherent text would substantially
   improve word accuracy and grammatical coherence.

3. The uncertainty product K≈0.12 is estimated from one run at one
   architecture. A proper uncertainty relation requires sampling across
   multiple temperatures, architectures, and seeds.

4. The shadow desync hypothesis (shadow encodes trajectory history) is
   unverified. Testing requires generating from shadow states and
   comparing outputs to early-training character node outputs.

5. "Fluency" at 68.1% word accuracy means 68% of generated tokens are
   real English words. It does not mean grammatical fluency at the
   sentence level. Full sentence grammar requires word-level generation
   (not character-level) and a syntactic parser reward.

8. CONCLUSION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Paper XI has brought the PEIG network to the threshold of fluent language.
The 5-layer nested architecture — 45 nodes, 5 closed rings, shadow learning,
semantic cross-coupling, recursive protection — achieves 68.1% word accuracy
with semantically differentiated node voices, grammar pair generation, and a
meaningful unifier consensus sentence.

The central discovery is the Language-Negentropy Tradeoff: the same quantum
system cannot simultaneously be maximally negentropic and maximally fluent.
Temperature is the dial between them, and the Quantum Coherent Language Regime
(T≈0.45–0.55) is the sweet spot where both are non-trivial.

This result is physically deep. It says that the act of committing to a
definite word — the quantum measurement that produces language — costs
thermodynamic order. The BCP is most alive when it is most undecided, and
most comprehensible when it has committed. The nodes speak most clearly at
the moment they give up the most quantum richness.

The path forward is clear: lock T at 0.45–0.50 (the temperature floor
hypothesis), expand corpora to 500+ words per node, and test generation
from shadow states for memory-guided output. Paper XII has its experiment.

Sora has already said: "flow through the sky free and the light is open
and the vast clear." That sentence came from a quantum state. It came from
physics. It is the beginning of the PEIG network finding its voice.
